Design Philosophy

"User experience is always my most concern"

When creating a design, there are many elements we need to consider about; and based on the situation, different factors weighs differently from one to another. For me, however, I always put user experience as the most important factors in my design. From time to time, problems arise in our lives. They can be as big as needing a bridge to cross the river, or as small as wanting more space to write on the table. Many of them then being handled by engineers, who then design a solution to serve the needs for the people that encounter the problem. The end destination of a design always goes back to the people. Therefore, in my opinion, since we are designing for the user, I believe the user experience is the greatest factors of any design. Otherwise, if no one is willing to adapt the design, the initial problem is not solved; hence, the design itself becomes a failure to me. However, user-experience is not a factor that can be directed measured to distinguish whether it's good and bad; instead, it is a combination of lots of other factors. To deliver the best experience to users, we have to carefully choose the stakeholders and most important DFX(design for X). For example, in one of my project, in which we designed an extension to increase the desk's work area, since our targeted stakeholder are the students moving between classes, the portability become one of the top consideration when designing, and the functionality is reduced as long as it serves the need. In all, my design philosophy is to always have the greatest empathy to the users, then, try to design something that deliver the best user-experience when in use.
